# Chapter 2: The Path of Awakening | 第二章 · 走向觉醒的"人之路"

> 第二天一早，我和文杰又去了那条小河边，我明明知道那块石头就在下面。可我就是看不清它真正的样子。前一天我就想去捞它，因为它在水里会闪一种很奇特的光。不是普通石头那种干巴巴的反光，而是一种朦胧的蓝，很像月光石，照白底子里，透出一点冷冷的蓝。我那时候甚至觉得，那可能是一块很特别的石头，和和氏壁一样的石头。我蹲下来，把手伸进水里，刚一碰到水，我就立刻缩回来了。太冷了。

The next morning, Wenjie and I went back to that little river. I knew perfectly well that the stone was down there. I just couldn't see its true form. The day before, I had wanted to fish it out, because underwater it gave off a very strange kind of light. Not the dull, dry reflection of an ordinary stone, but a hazy blue — much like moonstone, a cold blue glimmering through a pale white base. At the time, I even thought it might be something truly special — a stone like He Shi Bi (和氏壁), the legendary jade. I crouched down and reached into the water, but the instant my fingers touched it, I pulled my hand right back. It was freezing.

---

> 我很想得到它，但我不愿意付那个代价。所以第二天文杰看我还盯着它，笑着一步跳下水中，然后他就真的趴了下去，把手伸进水里，抓了几下，把那块石头捞了起来。石头拿到我面前的那一刻，我整个人都愣住了，因为它根本不是我想象中的样子。

I wanted it badly, but I wasn't willing to pay the price. So the next day, when Wenjie saw me still staring at it, he laughed, jumped straight into the water, then actually lay down and thrust his hand in, grabbed around a few times, and pulled the stone out. The moment he held it up in front of me, I froze — because it looked nothing like what I had imagined.

---

> 没有蓝光，没有神秘感，没有那种"好像藏着什么东西"的感觉。它只是一个很普通的石头，灰白色的底，上面有一些细腻的云母片，闪着一点碎光。

No blue glow. No sense of mystery. None of that feeling that "something must be hidden inside." It was just an ordinary stone — a grayish-white base with some fine flakes of mica catching bits of light.

---

> 我当时站在那条小河边的一条腐烂的松木边，盯着那块石头，看了很久。第一反应不是失望，而是开始思考理由。我在想，是不是因为水的折射，是不是因为这块云母片在水里的时候，被水面和流动拉长了蓝色。我越想越觉得这些解释都很合理。于是我小心翼翼把石头又放回水里。我想看看它是不是还会重新发出那种蓝光，但已没有。

I stood there by that little river, next to a rotting pine log, staring at the stone for a long time. My first reaction wasn't disappointment — it was to start constructing reasons. I wondered if it was the refraction of the water, if maybe the mica flakes had their blue stretched and amplified by the surface and the current when the stone was submerged. The more I thought about it, the more reasonable these explanations seemed. So I carefully placed the stone back in the water. I wanted to see if it would emit that blue glow again. But it didn't.

---

> 它回到水里以后，也没有再变成我第一眼看到的样子。我站在那里，突然一下子明白了一件事，那道蓝光，好像从来就不在石头上，也不在水里，它只在我心里。

Once it was back in the water, it never returned to what I had seen the first time. Standing there, I suddenly understood something: that blue light had never been on the stone, nor in the water. It had only ever existed inside my mind.

---

> 我前面那些拼命找出来的解释，并不是为了寻找真相，而是在努力证明——我之前看到的那个因果关系是对的。后来我越来越确信：人平时就是这样，我们先看见一个结果，然后拼命给它找原因，再给这个原因找证据。

All those explanations I had desperately pieced together weren't a search for truth — they were an effort to prove that the cause-and-effect I had originally perceived was correct. Over time, I became more and more certain: this is what people do. We see a result first, then scramble to find a cause, then find evidence to support that cause.

---

> 最后让自己相信：我没看错，我想对了，这个世界就是这样。可很多时候，你只要把石头再放回水里看一眼，那些理由就会自己碎掉。

In the end, we convince ourselves: I wasn't wrong, I was right, the world really is this way. But so often, all you have to do is put the stone back in the water and look again, and those reasons shatter on their own.

---

> 我发现了反复观察对比意义，才有了这本人选天选论。

It was discovering the power of repeated observation and comparison that gave birth to this book, *A Philosophy of Choice and Cost*.

---

> 记录非常重要。因为只有记录，才能把你"当时真的是怎么想的"留下来。过一段时间再回头看，你才知道你当时看的，到底是河底的石头，还是水面上的波纹。后来我把那块石头带回了家，因为这个石头，还捐弃了文杰远的索尼二手音乐手机我清楚的记得号叫做立信w800c，到今天，它还在我书桌上。看起来只是一块很普通的石头，但我觉得，它比很多东西都重要，因为我后来很多关于人的思考，其实都从那条小河边开始的。如果有一天你们见到它，大概也不会觉得它有什么特别。

Recording is crucial. Because only through recording can you preserve what you were *actually* thinking at the time. Then, after some time has passed, you look back — and only then do you know whether what you were seeing was the stone on the riverbed or just ripples on the surface. I ended up bringing that stone home. Because of it, I even gave up Wenjie's secondhand Sony Ericsson music phone — I remember clearly it was a w800c. To this day, the stone still sits on my desk. It looks like nothing more than an ordinary rock, but I believe it matters more than most things, because so much of my later thinking about human nature actually began at that little riverside. If you ever see it someday, you probably wouldn't think there's anything special about it.

---

> 但对我来说，它是一切的起点。

> But for me, it is where everything began.

---

> 也是从那时候开始，我第一次认真地想一件事：如果人的思想真的是一条河，那我到底要怎么观察自己的河呢？

It was also from that point on that I seriously considered something for the first time: if a person's mind really is a river, then how exactly do I observe my own?

---

> 后来那段时间，我开始认真天天写日记，我写得很认真，也真到写完以后，还会拿给文杰看完以后，会给我一些很真实的反应。他看完之后会更理解一点。

During the period that followed, I started writing in my journal every day. I wrote earnestly, and after finishing, I would even show it to Wenjie. He would give me very honest reactions. After reading, he would understand me a little better.

---

> 但有一天，他突然跟我说："你以后不要再把日记给我看了"

> But one day, he suddenly told me: "Don't show me your journal anymore."

---

> 那天我们正在走丹江口市第一人民医院楼下那个十字路口，我们正在走向神通网吧打穿越火线的路上我当时停了一下，问他为什么。他说了一段话，我至今记了很多年。

That day, we were crossing the intersection below Danjiangkou City First People's Hospital, on our way to Shentong Internet Café to play CrossFire. I stopped for a moment and asked him why. He said something I have remembered for many years.

---

> 他说："人对他人的表达其实都是很虚伪的。"

He said: "What people express to others is always, in some way, dishonest."

---

> 我当时很不理解。他说他小时候父母离婚，从小跟着爷爷奶奶长大。他爸爸经常骂他，他妈妈不好。他妈妈又经常跟他说，他爸爸很狠。

I didn't understand at the time. He told me that his parents divorced when he was young, and he grew up with his grandparents. His father would often badmouth his mother. His mother would often tell him his father was cruel.

---

> 他说："每个人说的都不完全是假话，但也不完全是真话，因为每个人说话的时候，心里都有自己的目的，都有自己想保留的东西，也都有自己想让别人相信的版本。"

He said: "Nothing anyone says is entirely a lie, but nothing is entirely the truth either. Because whenever anyone speaks, they have their own purpose in mind, their own things they want to protect, and their own version of the story they want others to believe."

---

> 然后他看着我说："所以你只要是写给别人看的，那它就已经不真实了。"

Then he looked at me and said: "So as long as you're writing for someone else to read, it's already not real."

---

> 我当时听完其实很愤怒的。我真心想把我对生命的思考发给他看，我觉得他是在否定我，我写这些东西，是真心真意的，我甚至觉得那是我真实的一部分，结果他说一句"都是虚伪的"

When I heard that, I was actually furious. I had genuinely wanted to share my reflections on life with him. I felt like he was dismissing me. I had written those things with complete sincerity — I even felt they were a real part of me. And then he just says, "It's all dishonest."

---

> 我当时完全不能接受，后来我们因为这件事吵了一次。再后来，大概有2个月，我们几乎赌气不再联系，那段时间，我也没有再给任何人看我的日记。后来我拿以前写过的东西，我发现他是对的，以前我写的时候，是想给他看的，我会不自觉安排语言，会不自觉地强调某些情绪。也会不自觉地隐藏一些东西。

I absolutely could not accept it. We had a fight over it. After that, for about two months, we stubbornly refused to contact each other. During that time, I didn't show my journal to anyone. Later, I went back and reread what I had written before, and I realized he was right. When I had been writing with the intention of showing it to him, I would unconsciously arrange my language, unconsciously emphasize certain emotions, and unconsciously hide certain things.

---

> 而现在，如果再写一次。我已经不会那样写了。那一刻我才突然明白。他说的其实是对的。只要是写给别人看的东西，它就已经开始变形了。从那天以后，我的日记就再也没有给任何人看过。但今天写给你们，其实也会有一些变化，我尽力的在控制这种变化，但尽力也只会使得变化继续变化。

Now, if I were to write it again, I wouldn't write it that way. In that moment, I finally understood. What he said was true. As soon as something is written for another person's eyes, it has already begun to distort. From that day on, I never showed my journal to anyone again. But writing this for you today — that too will inevitably involve some distortion. I'm doing my best to control it, but the very effort to control the distortion only causes the distortion to shift further.

---

> 好了你们准备好了进入我的河流世界走一条自己思想觉醒的人之路了吗？

> Are you ready now? Ready to enter the world of my river and walk your own path of intellectual awakening — the Way of Man?

---

> 我们现在开始：

Let's begin:

---

> 如果人的思想是一条河，那想看清河底，其实是一件很难的事，因为水一直在流

If the human mind is a river, then seeing clearly to the bottom is actually very difficult — because the water never stops flowing.

---

> 信息在流
>
> 情绪在流
>
> 欲望在流

> Information flows.
>
> Emotions flow.
>
> Desires flow.

---

> 很多人一辈子都只看见水面的这些借口 理由 伪装，却从来没有看见河底。慢慢地，我总结出，想看见河底的石头，大概有三种办法。

Many people spend their entire lives seeing only the surface — the excuses, the reasons, the disguises — and never once see the riverbed. Gradually, I came to the conclusion that there are roughly three ways to see the stones at the bottom.

---

> 第一种，叫捞石头——每天记录道痕写日记
>
> 第二种，叫潜水——深度思考冥想
>
> 第三种，叫改河道——彻底泯灭过去的自己

> The first is called Fishing for Stones (捞石头) — keeping a daily Dao Marks (道痕) journal.
>
> The second is called Diving (潜水) — deep contemplation and meditation.
>
> The third is called Redirecting the River (改河道) — completely dissolving the former self.

---

> 但对于绝大多数人来说，我只建议先坚持去做，第一种，不小看人之路，他的尽头和天之路，殊途同归，他就是：捞石头，因为它最慢，但也最安全，最小代价。

But for the vast majority of people, I only recommend starting with the first method. Don't underestimate the Way of Man — its destination converges with the Way of Heaven. And that method is: Fishing for Stones. Because it is the slowest, but also the safest, and costs the least.

---

> 捞石头是什么意思？就是你不急着把整条河都看穿，你只捞一块，今天捞一块，明天再捞一块，捞起来，看一眼，再放回去，再看一眼，记下来

What does Fishing for Stones mean? It means you don't rush to see through the entire river at once. You just fish out one stone. Today, fish out one. Tomorrow, fish out another. Pull it up, look at it, put it back, look again, and write it down.

---

> 你要的不是一下子看遍整条河里每一块石头，你也不是今天就要彻底改变自己。你要的，只是慢慢建立一种能力：

What you want is not to see every stone in the river all at once. Nor is it to completely transform yourself today. What you want is to slowly build a capacity:

---

> 看到水面上的样子，就知道下面大概压着什么。

> To see the surface of the water and know, roughly, what lies beneath.

---

> 看到水面上的样子，就知道下面大概压着什么。

> To see the surface of the water and know, roughly, what lies beneath.

---

> 这就是天机的种子。这不是神秘，更不是算命，而是你下意识看自己的河底，看得越来越准。那怎么捞？

This is the seed of Celestial Insight (天机). This is not mysticism, and it is certainly not fortune-telling. It is your subconscious ability to read your own riverbed with increasing accuracy. So how do you fish?

---

> 我后来给自己找到一个很简单的方法，就是每天在自己最累的时候，去写日记。我那时候本来就很喜欢运动，每天健身、跑步，身体状态也很好。后来我发现，跑步之后的那段时间特别适合捞石头。因为你跑完步以后，身体已经很累了，血液和供氧都往四肢和全身走，脑子反而不会像平时那样高速运转。

I eventually found a very simple method for myself: write in my journal every day at my most exhausted moment. At the time, I already loved exercise — working out, running every day, keeping my body in good shape. Later I discovered that the period right after a run was especially suited for Fishing for Stones. Because after a run, your body is already spent. Blood and oxygen flow to your limbs and throughout your body, and your brain actually stops running at its usual breakneck speed.

---

> 这个时候，河流会慢下来，不是彻底停，但会明显变慢，而水一慢，水面就会平静一些，平静一些，就更容易看到底。所以我后来给自己的方法是：

At that point, the river slows down. Not a complete stop, but a noticeable slowing. And when the water slows, the surface calms. And when it calms, it becomes easier to see the bottom. So the method I settled on for myself was:

---

> 跑步
>
> 洗澡
>
> 然后写

> Run.
>
> Shower.
>
> Then write.

---

> 怎么写非常重要。我之前指导我徒弟们写日记，他们大都在写一些，今天很累，今天不开心，今天谁让我失望，今天我想明白了什么？这些都是记录意义很低的，这些都是水面的波纹，我们要记录的是：道痕。

How you write matters enormously. When I used to guide my apprentices in journaling, most of them would write things like: "Today was exhausting," "Today I was unhappy," "Today someone disappointed me," "Today I figured something out." These entries have very little value as records. They are all just ripples on the surface. What we need to record is: Dao Marks.

---

> 不是记录生活。

Not a record of your life.

---

> 不是记录感受。

Not a record of your feelings.

---

> 不是记录故事。

Not a record of your stories.

---

> 而是每天从河里捞一块石头上来，每天怎么写，才不是写水面我建议每一篇"道痕"都固定按这个顺序写：

But rather, fishing out one stone from the river each day. How do you write in a way that isn't just skimming the surface? I suggest that every Dao Marks entry follow this fixed structure:

---

> **第一层：事实**

> **Layer One: The Facts**

---

> 先只写发生了什么，不许解释，不许评价，不许抒情，比如："今天他说我变了"，"今天我看到别人赚钱很快"，"今天我本来想回消息，后来没回"，"今天我看到一个人炫耀，我心里很不舒服"。这一层的作用，是把石头所在的位置先圈出来。很多人一上来就写道理，那样很容易把石头盖住。

Start by writing only what happened. No explaining, no evaluating, no emoting. For example: "Today he said I've changed." "Today I saw someone else making money fast." "Today I meant to reply to a message but didn't." "Today I saw someone showing off and it made me uncomfortable." The purpose of this layer is to mark the location of the stone. Many people start by writing principles and lessons — that only buries the stone deeper.

---

> **第二层：第一反应**

> **Layer Two: The First Reaction**

---

> 写你当时最直接的感觉，不是正确答案，是第一反应。比如："我当时很想反驳"，"我很酸"，"我有点怕"，"我突然很想证明自己"，"我一下子想逃"。这一层很重要，因为第一反应，往往最接近石头。

Write your most immediate, gut-level feeling. Not the "correct" answer — the first reaction. For example: "I wanted to argue back." "I felt jealous." "I was a bit scared." "I suddenly wanted to prove myself." "I wanted to run." This layer is critical, because the first reaction is usually the closest thing to the stone.

---

> **第三层：我想得到什么，这一步，是找贪婪。**

> **Layer Three: What do I want to get out of this? This step is about finding Greed (贪婪).**

---

> 你必须逼自己回答一句：如果我顺着这股劲往前走，我到底想得到什么。注意，不要写大词，不要写"尊严""幸福""意义"要写具体的。比如："我想让他承认我没错"，"我想比那个人强"，"我想让别人高看我"，"我想保住这段关系"，"我想拿到这个机会"，"我想确认自己不是失败者"。这一步一写，贪婪常就出来了。

Force yourself to answer one thing: if I follow this impulse forward, what am I actually trying to get? And don't use big, abstract words. Don't write "dignity" or "happiness" or "meaning." Write something specific. For example: "I want him to admit I wasn't wrong." "I want to be better than that person." "I want others to think highly of me." "I want to keep this relationship." "I want to seize this opportunity." "I want to confirm that I'm not a failure." Once you write this step, Greed usually reveals itself.

---

> **第四层：我在害怕什么**

> **Layer Four: What am I afraid of?**

---

> 这一步，是找恐惧。同样也不能写大词。要写具体，最小，最直接的那个怕。比如："我怕被看不起"，"我怕我其实没那么厉害"，"我怕失去他"，"我怕我选错了"，"我怕承认以后自己很蠢"，"我怕我其实一直在骗自己"。这一步特别重要，因为很多人只会写自己贪婪得到什么，不会写自己在怕什么，可真正让人缩手的，往往是这里。

This step is about finding Fear (恐惧). Again, no big words. Write the specific, smallest, most direct fear. For example: "I'm afraid of being looked down on." "I'm afraid I'm not actually that talented." "I'm afraid of losing them." "I'm afraid I chose wrong." "I'm afraid that admitting this would make me look stupid." "I'm afraid I've been lying to myself all along." This step is especially important, because many people can only write about what they greedily want — they can't write about what they fear. But what truly makes a person pull back their hand is usually found here.

---

> **第五层：我给自己找了什么理由**

> **Layer Five: What excuse did I make for myself?**

---

> 这一步是最关键的，这是找水面的波纹你要问自己：为了让自己舒服一点，我给这件事找了哪些解释。比如："我不是嫉妒，我只是客观分析"，"我不是害怕，我只是再等等"，"我不是不想要，我只是看得更远"，"我不是生气，我只是觉得不值得"，"我不是想证明自己，我只是说句公道话"。这一层，写的是：你是怎么自治的。很多人日记写到第三层就停了。因为真正的石头，往往藏在你最会讲道理的那些理由后面。所以永远不要停止捞石头，因为真正的石头，往往藏在你最会讲道理的那些理由后面。

This is the most critical step. This is about finding the ripples on the surface. Ask yourself: to feel a little more comfortable, what explanations did I construct for this situation? For example: "I'm not jealous — I'm just being objective." "I'm not afraid — I just want to wait a little longer." "It's not that I don't want it — I just see further ahead." "I'm not angry — I just don't think it's worth it." "I'm not trying to prove myself — I'm just speaking fairly." What this layer records is: how you engaged in Self-rationalization (自治). Many people stop their journals at the third layer. Because the real stones are usually hidden behind the reasons you're best at articulating. So never stop Fishing for Stones — the real stones are always hiding behind your most eloquent justifications.

---

> **第六层：今天捞出来的石头是什么**

> **Layer Six: What stone did I fish out today?**

---

> 最后只允许写一句定论：今天我捞出来的石头是：________只写一个主石头，不要又贪又怕写一堆。比如："今天主石头是：怕被看轻"，"今天主石头是：想证明自己更强"，"今天主石头是：想要确定性"，"今天主石头是：怕失去关系"，"今天主石头是：怕承认自己错了"。这一步非常关键，因为它逼你从一堆水面，落到一块石头。

At the end, you are only allowed to write a single conclusion: Today the stone I fished out is: ________. Write only one main stone. Don't get greedy and fearful and list a whole pile. For example: "Today's main stone is: fear of being undervalued." "Today's main stone is: wanting to prove I'm stronger." "Today's main stone is: wanting certainty." "Today's main stone is: fear of losing a relationship." "Today's main stone is: fear of admitting I was wrong." This step is critical, because it forces you to move from a mass of surface ripples down to a single stone.

---

> 为什么很多人写日记没用？因为他们只写前三层：发生了什么，我什么感觉。

Why do so many people find journaling useless? Because they only write the first three layers: what happened, how I felt.

---

> 我今天很委屈，这当然也有价值，但那只是"看见了水面"，真正能学会看自己的人，一定会多写两层：我到底想得到什么，我到底在害怕什么

"Today I felt wronged." Sure, that has some value — but it's only "seeing the surface." Anyone who truly learns to see themselves will always write two more layers: what do I actually want, and what am I actually afraid of?

---

> 然后再加一层：我为了保护自己，编了什么解释

Then add one more: what explanation did I fabricate to protect myself?

---

> 写到这里，你才开始真的摸到石头。为什么一定要回看？这个点你前面自己已经悟到了，也是最厉害的地方，石头捞上来，不是为了收藏，而是为了对比。

When you write to this point, you finally begin to actually touch the stone. And why must you go back and reread? You've already intuited this yourself — and that's the most powerful thing about it. Fishing out a stone isn't about collecting it. It's about comparison.

---

> 你今天捞起来看，明天再放回去看，过一周再看，过一个月再看，你会发现三件事：

You fish it out and look at it today. Tomorrow you put it back and look again. A week later, look again. A month later, look again. You will discover three things:

---

> **第一，你当时以为很大的事，后来根本不是那块石头。**
>
> **第二，你当时以为自己看得很准，其实全是自治。**
>
> **第三，某些石头会反复出现。**

> **First, what you thought was a big deal at the time turns out not to have been that stone at all.**
>
> **Second, what you thought you saw so clearly was actually nothing but Self-rationalization.**
>
> **Third, certain stones keep reappearing.**

---

> 这第三点最重要。如果一块石头一再出现，说明它不是偶发情绪，它是你河底的大石头。所以我建议你：每天写，每周回看一次，每月只找**"重复出现的三块石头"**。一个月后你会非常震惊。因为你会发现：你以为自己每天活得很复杂，其实翻来覆去，可能就被那几块石头推着走。

This third point is the most important. If a stone keeps showing up, it isn't a passing emotion — it is one of the great stones on your riverbed. So my recommendation is: write every day, reread once a week, and each month identify only **"the three stones that keep recurring."** After one month, you will be shocked. Because you will discover that the life you thought was so complex is actually being pushed along, over and over, by just a few stones.

---

> 怎么通过日记学会人选？这个地方才是重点，很多人以为"看见石头"就够了，其实不够。看见石头，只是第一步，真正的人选，是当你下一次又遇到同样的波纹时，你知道下面是哪块石头。

How do you use journaling to learn Human Choice (人选)? This is the real point. Many people think that "seeing the stone" is enough. It isn't. Seeing the stone is only the first step. True Human Choice is when the next time you encounter the same ripple, you know which stone lies beneath it.

---

> 比如以前你看到别人成功，会起波澜，你只会觉得自己难受，写了十天道痕以后，你会知道："哦，这不是单纯难受，这下面是想证明自己更强。"又或者："哦，这不是理性犹豫，这下面是怕承担代价。"

For example, before, when you saw someone else succeed, it would stir up waves. You would only feel a vague discomfort. But after ten days of writing Dao Marks, you would know: "Ah, this isn't just discomfort — underneath it is the desire to prove I'm stronger." Or: "Ah, this isn't rational hesitation — underneath it is the fear of bearing the cost."

---

> 这时候你就慢慢开始有"选"的资格了，但如何选大概还要写到30章以后了，最近发表了原文第一章开始，很多人就迫不及待的开始选，让我真的害怕。他们不愿意打基础，不愿意听原理，只想去选，这更加坚定了要把如何选祸放在更后面的位置。

At that point, you slowly begin to earn the right to "choose." But how to actually choose — that will probably have to wait until around Chapter 30. Shortly after I published the original Chapter 1, many people couldn't wait to start choosing, and that genuinely frightened me. They didn't want to build the foundation, didn't want to hear the principles — they just wanted to jump straight to choosing. That only made me more determined to place the methodology of choosing much further along in the book.

---

> "道痕"模板，你们可以直接用这个，每天写一页：
>
> **1.** 今天最起波澜的一件事是什么。
>
> **2.** 当时我的第一反应是什么。
>
> **3.** 我其实想得到什么。
>
> **4.** 我其实在害怕什么。
>
> **5.** 我给自己找了什么理由。
>
> **6.** 今天捞出来的主石头是什么。
>
> **7.** 如果明天再遇到同样的事，我准备怎么选。

> The Dao Marks template — you can use this directly, one page per day:
>
> **1.** What was the one thing today that stirred the most waves in me?
>
> **2.** What was my first reaction at the time?
>
> **3.** What did I actually want to get?
>
> **4.** What was I actually afraid of?
>
> **5.** What excuse did I make for myself?
>
> **6.** What is today's main stone?
>
> **7.** If the same thing happens tomorrow, how do I plan to choose?

---

> 因为前六句是在看石头，第七句，才开始接近模拟人选。有人批判我的人选天选论是玄学，看完了序他发现不是，现在开始批判说是废话，不可证明不可证伪，看完了这章，我们拭目以待，人选天选论是很实用的行动指南。

The first six questions are about examining the stone. The seventh is where you begin to simulate Human Choice. Some people have criticized my theory of Human Choice and Heaven's Selection as mysticism. After reading the preface, they realized it wasn't. Now they've shifted to calling it empty platitudes — unfalsifiable, unprovable. After finishing this chapter, let's wait and see. *A Philosophy of Choice and Cost* is a highly practical guide to action.

---

> 坚持捞下去吧，捞久了，你就会慢慢知道，什么是水。什么是波纹，什么是石头，什么是你自己。而当你开始知道这些的时候，你才算真正开始有了学习人选天选论的入场券。

Keep fishing. Fish long enough, and you will slowly come to know what is water, what are ripples, what are stones, and what is you. And when you begin to know these things, only then have you truly earned your ticket of admission to studying the theory of Human Choice and Heaven's Selection.

---

> **第二种方法，是潜水，**

> **The second method is Diving,**

---

> 也就是深度思考。这种方法很快，也很危险，我一定要把危险讲清楚，因为很多人一听到"深度思考"，就觉得那是一件特别高级的事。其实不是。它很容易把人带进虚无。我人生里有过两段很接近抑郁的经历，都和"潜水"有关。

which is to say, deep contemplation. This method is fast, and it is dangerous. I must be clear about the danger, because many people hear "deep contemplation" and assume it's something lofty and elevated. It isn't. It can very easily pull a person into the void. In my life, I have had two episodes that came close to depression, and both were related to Diving.

---

> 第一次，是我一个陪伴我很多年的朋友。后来得了肝癌，我那段时间不停地想死亡，想得太深，想得太大，差点走不出来。

The first time, a friend who had been by my side for many years was diagnosed with liver cancer. During that period, I couldn't stop thinking about death. I thought too deeply, too broadly, and I nearly couldn't find my way out.

---

> 第二次，是我后来研究人类基因结构的时候，我看见了一些很底层的东西，然后往下挖，挖着挖着，人会掉进一种虚空的地方。

The second time, I was studying the structure of human genetics. I glimpsed something very fundamental, and I kept digging deeper. The further I dug, the more a person can fall into a kind of emptiness.

---

> 所以我后来越来越确信：深度思考不是所有人都适合立刻做的。如果你还不会看自己的河，还不会分辨石头，还不会判断自己现在的状态。你一上来就潜水，很容易先把自己搞抑郁。所以这一章我不教你潜水，最多只是告诉你：它存在，很快，很危险。等把前面的结构慢慢看清楚了，再去潜，会安全很多。

So I became increasingly certain: deep contemplation is not something everyone is ready to do immediately. If you don't yet know how to read your own river, can't yet distinguish the stones, can't yet judge your own current state — and you dive straight in — you can very easily send yourself into depression first. So in this chapter, I won't teach you how to Dive. At most, I'll tell you: it exists, it's fast, and it's dangerous. Once the structures I've described earlier become clearer to you, diving will be much safer.

---

> **第三种方法，是改河道。**

> **The third method is Redirecting the River.**

---

> 这个最厉害，也最难。改河道，不是去看石头，而是直接改变河的流向。一条河一旦真的改道，原来的河床会有一段时间露出来，那时候，你看河底会看得非常清楚。所以改河道之前，其实你最好先知道自己原来的河底是什么。改道之后，再看一次，前后对照，那会非常震撼。

This is the most powerful, and the most difficult. Redirecting the River doesn't mean examining the stones — it means directly altering the river's course. Once a river truly changes course, the old riverbed is exposed for a time, and during that window, you can see the bottom with extraordinary clarity. So before you redirect, you should ideally already know what your original riverbed looks like. After the redirect, look again. Compare before and after — and the effect will be staggering.

---

> 但这种方法的代价很大；这里先不展开，因为一展开，很多人又会把它听成玄学。你只要先记住一句话就行：改河道很猛，但不是普通人该轻易触碰的东西。

But the cost of this method is enormous. I won't go into detail here, because the moment I do, many people will mistake it for mysticism again. Just remember one thing for now: Redirecting the River is powerful, but it is not something ordinary people should casually attempt.

---

> 如果一个人真的想认识自己，第一步不是深度思考，也不是改变结构。第一步就是安静地捞一块石头。然后每天捞一块，一点一点地，一点一点地对照。一点一点地校正自己那些看似合理，其实只是自治的判断。

If a person truly wants to know themselves, the first step is not deep contemplation, nor is it restructuring. The first step is to quietly fish out one stone. Then fish out one every day, bit by bit, comparing bit by bit, gradually correcting all those judgments that seemed so reasonable but were really just Self-rationalization.

---

> 你会慢慢发现：你为什么伸手，你为什么缩手，你为什么总是上同一种"蓝光"。又为什么每次拿起来以后，都发现那不过是一块普通的石头。当你开始做到这一步的时候，你才算真正开始认识自己的内心。

You will slowly discover: why you reach out, why you pull back, why you keep falling for the same kind of "blue glow." And why, every time you pick it up, you find it's nothing more than an ordinary stone. When you begin to do this, you have truly started to understand your own inner world.

---

> 20年过去了，那块石头，它一直放在我的桌子的紫砂壶旁边。他旁边还有一块蓝色的斯里兰卡月光石。有时候我会拿起来看着对比他们，它其实一点也不特别，灰白色，带一点云母的闪光。如果你在河边看到它，大概不会多看一眼。但我每次看到它，都会想起那条小河边，想起当年站在水边的那个自己。

Twenty years have passed, and that stone still sits on my desk, next to my Yixing clay teapot. Beside it rests a blue Sri Lankan moonstone. Sometimes I pick them up and compare them. The river stone is really nothing special — grayish-white, with a faint shimmer of mica. If you saw it by a riverbank, you probably wouldn't give it a second glance. But every time I see it, I think of that little river, and I think of the person I was, standing at the water's edge all those years ago.

---

> 引起这一切的都只是：贪婪，恐惧

> What set all of this in motion was nothing more than: Greed (贪婪) and Fear (恐惧).

---

> 下一章我带你们进入我一体两面的"硬币世界"你准备好了吗？在恐惧和贪婪中，万花丛中过片叶不沾身。

In the next chapter, I'll take you into my world of "the two-sided coin." Are you ready? To walk through a forest of Greed and Fear — and emerge without a single petal clinging to you.
